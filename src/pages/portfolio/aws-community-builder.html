AWS Community Builder

Portfolio of Evidence

Executive Summary

As an Active AWS Community Builder (Serverless), User Group Leader for AWS User Group Cape Town, and Chief Executive Officer and Founder of AWS Community South Africa (non-profit), I have built and scaled a community-driven ecosystem focused on practical enablement, inclusivity, and measurable impact across the AWS landscape in South Africa and broader Africa.

In 2025, I founded AWS Community South Africa, a non-profit initiative created to extend impact beyond a traditional user group model. The goal was to establish a structured, sustainable platform for community growth, skills development, and industry collaboration. Through this, I launched a mentorship program supporting currently 10 active mentees, created partnerships with AWS Partners to enable CSI-driven community initiatives, and positioned the community to participate in major developer ecosystems, including DevConf and Belgium Industry Connect, as a recognized community sponsor without any need for sponsorship fees. I have managed to position AWS Community South Africa as a community sponsor where we are a free sponsor at these events, but our members enjoy the benefits from these events.

As the AWS User Group Leader for Cape Town, I have consistently driven high engagement and growth through regular, high-quality events. I host monthly meetups averaging 60+ attendees, alongside larger flagship events such as AWS re:Invent re:cap (120 attendees) and Leadership Session with an international Amazonian (130 attendees)

A key milestone was organizing and leading the first-ever AWS Community Day in South Africa (Cape Town), which attracted 450 attendees. I led this initiative end-to-end, including delivering the opening keynote alongside the AWS Country Manager and Managing Director of Amazon South Africa — positioning the event as a cornerstone moment for the local AWS ecosystem.

To further drive engagement and retention, I built a custom community mobile application that gamifies participation, rewarding members with points redeemable for swag and exclusive items — reinforcing consistent involvement and community loyalty.

In my personal capacity as a technical advocate and speaker, I have delivered talks across multiple high-impact platforms:

  • Keynote Speaker at AWS Community Day South Africa (450 attendees)
  • AWS re:Invent re:cap Cape Town (120 attendees)
  • AWS Leadership Session with an international Amazonian (130 attendees)
  • Regular speaker at AWS User Group Cape Town meetups (70+ attendees per event)
  • .NET Conference 2025 (Johannesburg, Durban, Cape Town)
  • AWS Summit Johannesburg 2025 (2 sessions) and 2024
  • ISPIM Connects Cape Town 2025 (International conference)

In addition to live engagements, I actively contribute to the AWS ecosystem through consistent written technical content and thought leadership. I regularly publish blogs across platforms including Medium, LinkedIn, Dev.to, and the AWS Community Blog, where I share practical insights, architectural patterns, and real-world lessons learned from working with AWS technologies. My content focuses on bridging the gap between theory and implementation, enabling builders to apply AWS services effectively in their own environments. Through consistent publishing and cross-platform reach, I extend my impact beyond in-person engagements, contributing to the global AWS knowledge-sharing community and supporting continuous learning at scale.

Looking ahead to 2026, my focus is on scaling impact across Africa through structured and repeatable initiatives:

  • Quarterly hands-on technical workshops
  • Leadership and growth sessions to inspire the next generation of builders
  • Gamified learning experiences through community game nights
  • Expansion of AWS Community Day into two cities (Cape Town and Johannesburg)
  • Hosting Africa’s first AWS Community GameDay (second globally)
  • Launching training and certification programs to accelerate skills development at scale

What differentiates my contribution is a strong focus on execution, scalability, and ecosystem building — not just running events, but creating a movement that enables continuous growth, learning, and participation.

My goal as an AWS Hero is to amplify this impact further, contribute at a global level, and continue positioning Africa as a high-growth, high-impact region within the AWS community.
